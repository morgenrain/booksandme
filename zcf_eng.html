To promote mastery of Chinese language, culture and identity among overseas Chinese children in North America. Facilitate harmonious exchange and interaction among young Chinese children and parents world-wide.

Ethnic Chinese are historically a highly migrant people, actively emigrating to adopted countries on one hand, yet maintaining a strong desire for homeland connections on the other. Modern Chinese immigrants tend to adopt a country after graduate study, and are hence better educated, and sometimes more Westernized than forebears who made their way via labor or trade. This Westernization leaves new immigrant parents at a disadvantage when attempting to raise a newborn the traditional way in an adopted country. Overseas Chinese schools help with language and culture education, but focus predominantly on primary school-age children or later; structured support is lacking for new parents to-be through pre-kindergarten age children. The Zhaoyu Cultural Foundation seeks to support parents and children going through this stage of life through focused integration of global Chinese media and community resources.

  • "Books & Me" Children's Chinese Library
    • Located in Silicon Valley, "Books & Me" is a physical library that will aggregate Chinese-language books, software and other media for young children. The library's collection will focus on media for young children and parents/parents-to-be gathered from the Greater China region.

  • Children's Virtual Library (CVL)
    • The CVL extends the benefit of CLS beyond regional constraints to benefit the larger overseas community. Using the physical media collection at the CCL as a base, the CVL will provide a rich online catalog indexed by vocabulary, theme/concepts, age group and community reviews.



    • Vicky Huang, Co-founder and Board member
      Vicky Huang co-founded the Zhaoyu Cultural Foundation in late 2003 with Jack Hong to promote Chinese language and culture learning among overseas Chinese children. Vicky has over ten years working experience in marketing and consulting fields, including Senior Marketing Manager at TalkCity and Director of Marketing at SINANET.com. Before that, she spent years in greater China, working for companies like Anderson Consulting, Procter & Gamble, Citibank and IBM. Vicky holds a BS in International Trade from National Taiwan University and an MBA from Stanford University.

    • Gina Ng, Library Director
      Gina Ng is the Head of the Foundation's Library project. She supervises the planning, purchasing, processing, cataloging, and set-up of Books & Me Chinese Children's Library, the first public service provided by the Zhaoyu Cultural Foundation. Gina has more than ten years working experience at various libraries across United States, including 8 years as a librarian at American Jewish Historical Society. Gina holds a BA in Library Science from National Taiwan University and a master's degree in Library Science from University of Wisconsin, Madison.
